IS 590R – AI Applications in Business (Winter 2026)
Brigham Young University – Marriott School of Business (MISM)
Course Description
This course prepares students to operate effectively in an AI-accelerated professional world. It
emphasizes problem identification, systems thinking, clear communication, collaboration, and the
design of practical AI-powered solutions. AI is treated as a thinking partner, not a shortcut.
Learning Outcomes
Students will learn to identify high-quality problems, apply systems thinking, communicate with
influence, collaborate across stakeholders, understand AI architectures in business, exercise strong AI
judgment, and integrate these skills into a meaningful end-to-end project.
Grading Breakdown
Participation & Engagement: 20%
Reading Reflections & Synthesis: 20%
Individual Assignments & Labs: 20%
Team Project (semester-long): 40%
Team Project Note
Each student will submit short peer-contribution summaries. Consistent under-contribution may result in
adjusted individual credit.
AI Usage Policy
AI is encouraged for ideation, prototyping, and coding. AI is not permitted for personal reflections
unless explicitly stated. Disclosure of AI usage is required.
Attendance & Integrity
Because the course is discussion-based, attendance is expected. Students must follow BYU Honor
Code and academic integrity standards.
Final Note
This course is designed to upgrade how you think, not just what you know.
